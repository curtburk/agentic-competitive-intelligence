## Overview

The NVIDIA DGX Spark is described as the 'smallest AI supercomputer NVIDIA has ever built' and a 'personal AI supercomputer.' It is a compact desktop system designed for AI developers, researchers, and data scientists who need to prototype, fine-tune, and run inference on large models locally. It is positioned between consumer AI laptops and cloud GPU clusters, allowing users to build AI agents and solutions before migrating to DGX cloud or data centers.

## HP ZGX Comparison

The DGX Spark competes with the HP ZGX Nano. Both are compact, high-performance AI workstations. The DGX Spark utilizes a unified memory architecture (GB10 Superchip) to run large models locally, whereas the ZGX Nano typically relies on discrete GPUs. The DGX Spark is positioned as a 'personal AI supercomputer' for developers, while the ZGX Nano is part of HP's broader professional workstation lineup.
