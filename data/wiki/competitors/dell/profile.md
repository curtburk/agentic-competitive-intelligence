## Overview

An AI supercomputer built for the desk, designed for AI developers and enterprises looking to run data-center class AI models locally to cut cloud costs and ensure data security. It is positioned as the 'real deal' desktop version of the chips used in NVIDIA's 'AI factories,' capable of running trillion-parameter models for inference, fine-tuning, and agentic workflows.

## Products

- **Dell Pro Max GB300** (Fury competitor) - Direct competitor to ZGX Fury
- **Dell Pro Max GB10** (Nano competitor) - Direct competitor to ZGX Nano

## Their Positioning

The AI supercomputer built for your desk

## HP ZGX Comparison

Competes directly with the HP ZGX Fury. Key differentiators for Dell include the use of the NVIDIA Grace Blackwell Ultra Superchip (Arm-based CPU + GB300 GPU) versus HP's likely x86/NVIDIA discrete GPU configurations, and the inclusion of a custom ethylene glycol cooling system to handle the 1600W TDP in a desktop form factor. Dell positions this as bringing 'data-center class' hardware to the desk for trillion-parameter model inference and fine-tuning.
